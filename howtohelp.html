How to Help

Educate

Educate yourself and others about what is happening and how to help. Hate crimes against people of color come from a long history of racism and are still prevalent today.

Volunteer

Contact your local organizations to find out if there are any ways to volunteer. If there are none nearby, email or call online organizations to still be involved.

Sign

Signing petitions that support the cause are one of the easiest ways to help!

Donate

If you are able to, donate to organizations that support the movement against racism. This allows them to strengthen their racism response network. There are also many fundraisers that help families effected by hate crimes.

Speak Up

Whenever you see a hate crime or something suspicious, speak up! But, be sure to be in a position to speak up before you confront anyone. Try to record everything and remember any details that may help in describing the situation.

Report

If you witnessed a hate crime or were a victim of one, report the incident to a trusted organization's helpline or 9-1-1. This allows us to help you and the community maintain a safer environment. You may use the call script below as a guide for future reports.
